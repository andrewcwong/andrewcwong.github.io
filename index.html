ABOUT ME

Hi there, thanks for stopping by! You've stumbled upon my personal page so let me share with you a bit about myself. My name is Andrew Wong and I'm a 4th year Chemical Engineering student, minoring in Management and Society. Through my journey of life I've gathered a multitude of experiences which has shaped and inspired me to pursue a career in Product Management.

A snapshot of some of the things I've done: (a) Led a cross-functional team for a $1 million secret government waste processing project, (b) Marketed and grew my university's first ever sparring club into one of the largest by revenue, (c) Consulted an oil and gas giant on data visualization automation, and currently, (d) Improving engineering change processes inside a nuclear reactor!

This website was designed by me for fun.

Hear my story

My humble beginnings

I went into engineering because I was fascinated with it was possible to use abstract principles of math and science to create ingenious products that solved day-to-day challenges. Chemical Engineering taught me to think critically, understand processes from a big picture, and manipulate numbers to maximize efficiencies.

Pictured: Overview of a $2.1 billion cryogenic carbon capture plant I designed

Gaining industry experience

My first professional internship in Project Management exposed me the cross-functional teams that worked together towards a billion dollar project, from design, to engineering, to operations, to senior management. It allowed me to appreciate the art of taking charge, aligning stakeholders, and driving the project vision to success.

Pictured: Inside of a CANDU nuclear reactor

Building my business acumen

This inspired me to learn more about the business side of things, where I took project-based courses in entrepreneurship and business strategy (including a global C2C classifieds business), did some pro bono consulting for Canada's largest oil & gas company, and even took home 1st place in my school's premier Management Consulting case competition, a team of engineering students against business and MBA students.

Pictured: The winning team (me, far right)

Road to Product Management

Around the same time, my interest in tech led me to Product Management, which I found to be the perfect intersect between engineering, business, and UX. I quickly realized as a PM I needed to better speak the language of software engineers, on top of my fundamentals of C/C++, VBA, and Matlab I used in school. To-date, I have self-taught myself Python, HTML/CSS, JavaScript, among others languages and libraries. I've also Marketing and Design tools; such as Google Analytics for data analytics and Axure RP for wireframing.
